The Fastest Growing Sport in America

What is Pickleball?

Pickleball is a paddle sport that brilliantly combines elements of tennis, badminton, and ping-pong. Played on a badminton-sized court with a slightly modified tennis net, it has become one of the fastest-growing sports in the United States — with over 36 million players by 2024.

The sport was invented in 1965 on Bainbridge Island, Washington, when three fathers — Joel Pritchard, Bill Bell, and Barney McCallum — improvised a backyard game for their bored kids using ping-pong paddles, a wiffle ball, and a lowered badminton net. What started as a family improvisation became a global phenomenon.

💡 Fun Fact: The sport's unusual name supposedly comes from the Pritchard family dog, "Pickles," who would relentlessly chase the ball during early games — though some accounts dispute this charming origin story!

Whether you're 8 or 80, pickleball is remarkably accessible. The smaller court means less ground to cover, the underhand serve is easy to learn, and the perforated plastic ball travels slower than a tennis ball — creating longer, more social rallies and a wonderfully welcoming community.

Rules & Strategy

Pickleball has four core rule categories. Each contains specific rules every player needs to master to compete effectively:

  1. Serving Rules
    • The serve must be underhand — paddle contact must be below the waist
    • The serve is hit diagonally cross-court into the opponent's service box
    • Only one serve attempt is allowed (except when the ball clips the net cord)
    • The server must keep both feet behind the baseline during the entire serve
    • In doubles, both partners on a side get to serve before possession changes
  2. The Kitchen — Non-Volley Zone
    • The "kitchen" is a 7-foot zone extending from the net on both sides of the court
    • You cannot volley (strike the ball before it bounces) while standing in the kitchen
    • You may enter the kitchen to play a ball that has already bounced in it
    • Momentum carrying you into the kitchen after a volley is also a fault
    • The kitchen is the defining feature that separates pickleball strategy from tennis
  3. Scoring
    • Standard games are played to 11 points, and you must win by 2
    • Tournament games sometimes go to 15 or 21 points (also win by 2)
    • In traditional scoring, only the serving side can earn a point
    • In rally scoring (used in some pro leagues), every rally earns a point
    • In doubles, the score is called as three numbers: server score, receiver score, server number (1 or 2)
  4. The Double Bounce Rule
    • After the serve, each side must let the ball bounce once before volleying
    • This means the serve must bounce and the return of serve must bounce
    • After those two mandatory bounces, either team may volley or play off a bounce
    • This rule prevents the serving team from immediately rushing and dominating the net
    • It creates a natural "dinking" phase at the start of each rally — a hallmark of the game
